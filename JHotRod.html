Intro

My dream car, the 1986 Toyota Sprinter or known in the car community by its chassis code, the AE86, is very much the car that inspires me to be who I am today. Some of the car’s traits can be described as simple, authentic, the underdog, and an old friend that you can come back to. For this project, I wanted to do a modern reinterpretation that blends neo-vintage aesthetic with references to the car’s actual history. The method of showing this is a final 3D visualization as it is a skill I am actively working on improving.

How can I embody my personal style into a car?

Research

Looking at the AE86 history, it raced in the world Touring Car Championship in 1986 and I liked how the car stands with a functional racing stance. The other side to the car’s history is street drifting in the 90s and those kinds of AE86 had exaggerated styling of the body panels. Looking back at the mood board that inspired my current style, I had a lot of hot rods that served as inspiration. This led me to thinking about Magnus Walker's outlaw 911's which had a clean but grungy appearance similar to the hot rods.

Ideation

Looking at my personal style, I can see 50s Californian hot rod influences and the closes I’ve seen that replicated on a car with a shape we know today is an Outlaw Porsche 911. So why not run with the theme of a “Japanese Hot Rod”. The look and feel is like the hot rods in California but the form is very much 80s Japanese race car.

First Draft

To save time on this project I bought a pre existing 3D model of the AE86 and I modelled a simple body kit on top of it. I didn’t want to completely change the style of the car but more of an enhancement.

Final Presentation

I initially was not gonna model a 3D scene for the car but I felt that to bring the theme out even further, a backdrop was needed. I took inspiration from the industrial buildings in California and made a simple background for the car to be set in.

Things I learned

• Use the project purpose statement to guide decisions.
